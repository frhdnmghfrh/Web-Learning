What is a Phrase?

A phrase is a group of words that convey a single meaning but do not have a subject + verb pattern, and therefore cannot stand alone as a complete sentence. Phrases usually consist of two or more words and function as parts of a sentence, such as the subject, object, complement, or adverbial.

  • Noun phrase: a beautiful house
  • Adjective phrase: very tall
  • Adverbial phrase: very quickly
  • Verb phrase: is running
  • Prepositional phrase: in the room

What is an English Expression?

An expression in English is a phrase or group of words used to express emotions, opinions, or specific situations in a unique or idiomatic way. These expressions often have special meanings that differ from the literal meanings of the individual words.

  • I'm on cloud nine – I’m very happy
  • Take it easy – Relax
  • Break the ice – Start a conversation comfortably
  • Costs an arm and a leg – Very expensive

Tips for Using Popular Phrases & Expressions

  • Use them according to the context: formal or informal.
  • Practice them in conversations to sound more natural.
  • Pay attention to tone and facial expressions when using them.
  • Listen to native speakers through movies, podcasts, or videos.
