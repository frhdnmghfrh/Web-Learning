English Sentence Structure & Grammar

Understanding sentence structure and grammar is a fundamental foundation in constructing correct and effective English sentences. Below are complete explanations and practical examples that are easy to understand.

Basic Sentence Structure

Sentence PatternExplanationExample SentenceTranslation
SVOSubject + Verb + ObjectShe reads a book.Dia membaca sebuah buku.
SVSubject + VerbHe sleeps.Dia tidur.
SVCSubject + Verb + ComplementShe is a teacher.Dia adalah seorang guru.
SVASubject + Verb + AdverbThey work here.Mereka bekerja di sini.

Types of Sentences Based on Structure

Type of SentenceExplanationExample SentenceTranslation
Simple SentenceA sentence with one independent clauseShe washed her car.Dia mencuci mobilnya.
Compound SentenceTwo independent clauses connected by a conjunctionShe went home and John stayed at the office.Dia pulang dan John tetap di kantor.
Complex SentenceOne independent clause + one dependent clauseJoni finished cooking even though he was late.Joni selesai memasak meskipun dia terlambat.
Compound-ComplexTwo independent clauses + one dependent clauseEven though I slept early, I woke up late and missed school.Meskipun saya tidur awal, saya bangun terlambat dan bolos sekolah.

Basic English Grammar Sentence Patterns

Sentence PatternExample SentenceTranslation
S + V + OI drink coffee.Saya minum kopi.
S + V + Adv + OShe reads quickly.Dia membaca dengan cepat.
S + V + AdjHe looks tired.Dia terlihat lelah.
S + to be + ComplementThey are students.Mereka adalah siswa.
S + Modal + VShe can swim.Dia bisa berenang.

Parts of Speech in Grammar

Part of SpeechExample WordsFunction in Sentence
Nounbook, car, happinessRepresents a person, place, thing, or idea
Verbrun, eat, beShows action or state
Adjectivebig, happy, beautifulDescribes a noun
Adverbquickly, very, wellDescribes a verb, adjective, or another adverb
Pronounhe, she, it, theyReplaces a noun
Prepositionin, on, at, underShows the relationship between a noun and other words
Conjunctionand, but, because, althoughConnects words, phrases, or clauses
Interjectionoh!, wow!, ouch!Expresses feeling or reaction

Using Conjunctions

Type of ConjunctionExample WordsFunctionExample Sentence
Coordinatingand, but, or, so, yetConnects equal clausesI like tea and she likes coffee.
Subordinatingbecause, although, while, when, ifConnects a main clause with a subordinate clauseI stayed home because it was raining.
Correlativeeither...or, neither...nor, both...andConnects two parallel elementsBoth John and Mary are coming to the party.

Tips to Master Sentence Structure and Grammar

  • Learn the basic sentence patterns first (SVO, SV, SVC, SVA).
  • Pay attention to subject-verb agreement (singular subjects use singular verbs, plural use plural).
  • Use conjunctions properly to form more complex and varied sentences.
